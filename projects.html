Pantry Raider

Pantry Raider's idea arose from one of our team members' past careers. Like many other students in my cohort, we were career transitioning into becoming Full Stack developers. Joey was transitioning his career from being a chef to becoming a developer. We created this app to help anyone research recipes based on the ingredients they have available.

Technologies

The group used Redux and Express to handle the backend. We used Sass to stylize our pages and Ejs to render these pages.

Challenges

Use Sequelize to construct the database and connect the relationships between modals. Some challenges were learning Sequelize in a couple of days and reconstructing the database multiple times during development.

Plant Nursery

Plant Nursery is an application that helps keep track of plants. My role was to be project leader for my team. As a leader, I would have to present each morning with each member's progress update, stay in contact with my teammates to check for any blockers, and give out a task. I did create the foundation of the app and decided on which technologies we would use.

Technologies

Using Sequelize, Express, and Redux, we used our API to POST for every new plant. Once in the database, we used React, HTML, and components to render the plants.

Challenges

A challenge was rendering the events on the calendar. Dealing with dates is a challenge as every software interprets time and date stamps differently. After handling the issue, I understood new debugging methods and a new function for dates. The function compares two different time stamps and returns the most recent timestamp.

Dolce Dessert

Once I knew that I wanted to be a web developer, I had promised my sister her website for Dolce Desserts, her small bakery. The website is a store for Dolce Dessert's customers to look at the menu and place orders. Giving consistency to my sister as all her placed orders are all in one place. This project is a work in progress, but it is my proudest creation as I see a reflection of how far I've come since I made that promise.

Technologies

For Dolce Dessert, I used React as my frontend framework. Express to handle my routes. Use React-Bootstrap and Sass for styling and components.

Challenges

Currently, I do not have a backend, but I will use Sequelize to store info about the user and their orders. More tasks are to add a page to display the past and current orders the user has. Another page for their profile and finish the functionality of the store.
